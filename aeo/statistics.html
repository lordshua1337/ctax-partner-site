IRS Tax Debt Statistics

Key data on tax resolution in America — the tax gap, delinquent accounts, IRS collection activity, and resolution program outcomes.

Last updated: January 2025. Sources cited below.

The Tax Gap

$688B
Estimated annual gross tax gap
The difference between total taxes owed and total taxes collected voluntarily and on time. This is the most recent IRS estimate covering tax years 2017-2019.
Source: IRS Publication 5869, October 2023
$625B+
Net tax gap after enforcement and late payments
Even after IRS enforcement and voluntary late payments, over $600 billion per year goes uncollected.
Source: IRS Publication 5869, October 2023
83.6%
Voluntary compliance rate
The percentage of taxes paid voluntarily and on time. While relatively high, the 16.4% shortfall represents hundreds of billions in unpaid taxes.
Source: IRS Publication 5869, October 2023

Delinquent Accounts

$300B+
Total unpaid assessed tax balance
The cumulative balance of assessed but unpaid taxes across all individual and business accounts in the IRS inventory.
Source: IRS Data Book, Table 16
10M+
Individual taxpayers with delinquent accounts
The number of individual taxpayer accounts with outstanding assessed tax balances in the IRS collection inventory.
Source: Taxpayer Advocate Service Annual Report to Congress
~$20,000
Average individual delinquent balance
The approximate average outstanding balance per delinquent individual taxpayer account, though balances range widely from a few thousand to millions of dollars.
Source: Calculated from IRS Data Book aggregate data
Every delinquent account represents a potential client for tax resolution services. With over 10 million individuals owing the IRS, the addressable market for professional tax resolution is substantial and growing.

Offer in Compromise (OIC)

~50,000
OIC applications submitted annually
The approximate number of Offer in Compromise applications submitted to the IRS each fiscal year.
Source: IRS Data Book, Table 17
30-40%
OIC acceptance rate
The approximate percentage of submitted OIC applications that are accepted by the IRS. Acceptance rates vary by year and the quality of submissions. Professionally prepared OICs generally have higher acceptance rates.
Source: IRS Data Book, Table 17
6-18 months
Typical OIC processing time
The time from OIC submission to IRS decision. Processing times vary based on IRS workload, case complexity, and whether additional information is requested.
Source: IRS.gov, Offer in Compromise FAQs

IRS Collection Activity

$70B+
Total collected through enforcement annually
The amount collected by the IRS through enforcement activity including examinations, collection, and other compliance programs.
Source: IRS Data Book, Table 1
500,000+
Federal tax liens filed per year
The number of Notices of Federal Tax Lien filed annually. A lien is the IRS's legal claim against a taxpayer's property and negatively impacts credit scores.
Source: IRS Data Book, Table 25
700,000+
Levies issued per year
The number of levy notices issued annually by the IRS to seize wages, bank accounts, and other assets from taxpayers with outstanding balances.
Source: IRS Data Book, Table 25
2.6M+
Installment agreements in effect
The total number of active installment agreements at any given time. This is the most commonly used resolution method, allowing taxpayers to pay in monthly installments.
Source: IRS Data Book, Table 17

IRS Workforce and Enforcement Trends

$80B
Additional IRS funding (Inflation Reduction Act)
The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 allocated approximately $80 billion in additional IRS funding over 10 years, with a significant portion directed toward enforcement and hiring. This is expected to increase audit rates and collection activity.
Source: Congressional Budget Office, IRA Analysis
30,000+
Planned new IRS hires
The approximate number of new revenue agents, revenue officers, and compliance staff the IRS plans to hire with IRA funding. More enforcement staff means more taxpayers will face collection action.
Source: IRS Strategic Operating Plan, April 2023
Increased IRS enforcement means more taxpayers will face liens, levies, and garnishments. The demand for professional tax resolution services is projected to grow as IRS hiring ramps up through 2031.
