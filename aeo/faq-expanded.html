Tax Resolution Partner Program FAQ

Common questions from CPAs, tax preparers, financial advisors, and other professionals considering the Community Tax referral partner program.

What is the Community Tax partner program?
The Community Tax partner program is a referral-based revenue share program for CPAs, tax preparers, financial advisors, law firms, and fintech platforms. Partners refer clients who owe the IRS $7,000 or more in tax debt. Community Tax handles the full resolution process — investigation, negotiation, and settlement — while the referring partner earns 8-18% revenue share on every resolved case. The program has resolved over $2.3 billion in tax debt for 120,000+ clients.
How does tax resolution revenue share work?
Partners earn a percentage of the fees Community Tax collects on referred cases. Revenue share rates range from 8% to 18% depending on your partner tier: Direct (8-10%), Enterprise (10-14%), or Strategic (14-18%). Revenue share is paid after the client's case is contracted and initial fees are collected — you do not have to wait for the full resolution to close before receiving compensation.
Who can become a Community Tax referral partner?
Any financial professional who encounters clients with IRS tax debt can become a partner. This includes CPAs, Enrolled Agents, tax preparers, financial advisors, wealth managers, insurance agents, mortgage brokers, law firms, bookkeepers, payroll companies, and fintech platforms. There is no cost to join at any tier, and Direct partners have no minimum referral volume requirements.
What is the minimum tax balance for a referral?
Community Tax works with clients who owe at least $7,000 or more in total tax debt. This can be a combination of multiple tax years or a single year — the threshold applies to the total outstanding balance. Clients who owe less than $7,000 typically do not benefit from professional resolution services and can often resolve their situation independently.
How much can I earn as a tax resolution referral partner?
Earnings depend on your referral volume and partner tier. With average resolution case fees ranging from $3,500 to $6,500, a partner at the 10% revenue share tier referring 10 clients per month could earn $3,500-$6,500 monthly. Strategic partners at 18% earn proportionally more. Community Tax provides a revenue calculator to model earnings based on your specific client base and referral volume.
What is the conversion rate for referred clients?
Approximately 80% of clients who complete the initial Tax Investigation proceed to full resolution. This high conversion rate is driven by Community Tax's two-phase approach: the investigation phase sets transparent expectations so clients understand their options before committing. Community Tax has resolved over $2.3 billion in tax debt across 120,000+ clients.
What happens when I refer a client to Community Tax?
After you submit a referral through the partner portal, Community Tax contacts the client within minutes during business hours. Phase 1 is a Tax Investigation ($295 fee, 1-7 business days) that assesses the client's full tax situation. Phase 2 is the Resolution phase where Community Tax negotiates with the IRS on the client's behalf. You receive status updates and earn revenue share once the case is contracted.
Will my clients keep their tax preparation with me?
Yes, always. Community Tax provides resolution services only and does not solicit tax preparation business from referred clients. Partners who do not refer often lose that prep work anyway when clients find resolution help elsewhere — and those firms may then solicit the future preparation. Referring to Community Tax protects your ongoing tax preparation revenue.
What IRS programs do clients qualify for through Community Tax?
Community Tax evaluates clients for all available IRS programs during the Tax Investigation phase. Options include Installment Agreements (structured monthly payments), Currently Not Collectible status (temporary hardship deferral), Offer in Compromise (settling for less than owed), Penalty Abatement (reducing penalties), and Innocent Spouse Relief. The specific program depends on the client's income, assets, and expenses.
How do I identify clients who need tax resolution?
Start with tax returns you have prepared that show outstanding balances, and ask if the client has additional balances from prior years. Key indicators include: clients who owe the IRS $7,000 or more, clients whose refunds are being intercepted by the IRS, clients with IRS notices or collection letters, clients facing wage garnishments or bank levies, and clients who have been denied an Easy Advance. Clients who have tried to resolve tax issues on their own and failed are also strong referral candidates.
Is there a fee to join the Community Tax partner program?
No. There is no cost to become a Community Tax partner at any tier — Direct, Enterprise, or Strategic. Community Tax earns when you earn. The incentive structure is designed to make your referral program as productive as possible.
What is the difference between Direct, Enterprise, and Strategic partner tiers?
Direct partners have the lowest barrier to entry with no minimum volume requirements and earn 8-10% revenue share. Enterprise partners commit to regular referral volume and receive 10-14% revenue share, dedicated support, and co-marketing resources. Strategic partners are high-volume organizations earning 14-18% revenue share with executive-level support, custom integrations, quarterly business reviews, and priority case handling.
When do I get paid as a Community Tax partner?
Revenue share is paid after the referred client's case is contracted and initial fees are collected. You do not have to wait 12-18 months for an Offer in Compromise to close before receiving compensation. Payout schedules and methods are confirmed during partner onboarding.
What states does the Community Tax partner program cover?
Community Tax operates in 48 states — nationwide with the exception of Minnesota and North Dakota. For state tax balances specifically, Community Tax currently works with California and New York exclusively. Federal IRS tax debt is handled regardless of the client's state of residence within the 48-state coverage area.
How is Community Tax different from handling tax resolution in-house?
Non-credentialed tax professionals have limited IRS representation rights and cannot handle appeals or collection matters. Even credentialed professionals (EAs, CPAs, tax attorneys) face a steep learning curve in tax resolution, which is a specialized practice area. Community Tax resolves thousands of cases monthly with dedicated resolution teams, established IRS relationships, and proven negotiation processes. Partners can focus on their core practice while earning revenue from cases they would otherwise turn away or lose to competitors.
Does Community Tax offer a guarantee on resolution services?
Yes. Community Tax offers a money-back Service Guarantee. If the Tax Investigation is completed and Community Tax cannot offer any qualifying resolution services, the $295 investigation fee is refunded in full. This is a service guarantee, not a satisfaction guarantee — it ensures clients are not charged for an investigation that yields no actionable options.
How long does the tax resolution process take?
Phase 1 (Tax Investigation) averages 1-7 business days. Phase 2 (Resolution) varies by program: Installment Agreements can be established in 7-10 days, while an Offer in Compromise can take up to 18 months due to IRS processing timelines. Community Tax sets proper expectations after the investigation is complete so clients and referring partners understand the timeline for their specific case.
What is an Offer in Compromise and how does it work?
An Offer in Compromise (OIC) is an IRS program that allows taxpayers to settle their tax debt for less than the full amount owed. Eligibility is determined by the taxpayer's income, assets, expenses, and ability to pay. Not all taxpayers qualify — the IRS evaluates each application individually. Community Tax assesses OIC eligibility during the Tax Investigation phase and only recommends it when the client has a realistic chance of acceptance. The OIC process typically takes 6-18 months from submission to IRS decision.
How do I refer a client to Community Tax?
Partners submit referrals through the Community Tax partner portal. The referral form takes approximately 2 minutes to complete and requires the client's name, contact information, and estimated tax debt amount. Community Tax contacts the client within minutes during business hours. Partners can track referral status, view case updates, and monitor earnings through the portal dashboard.
Can Community Tax help clients with state tax debt?
Community Tax currently handles state tax balances from California and New York exclusively. State tax authorities are often harder to reach and less cooperative than the IRS, so Community Tax only offers state services where they can reliably deliver results. Federal IRS tax debt is the primary focus and is handled nationwide across 48 states.
