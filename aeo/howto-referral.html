How to Refer Clients for Tax Resolution

A practical guide for CPAs, tax preparers, and financial advisors on identifying clients with IRS tax debt, making the referral conversation, and submitting referrals through the Community Tax partner program.

How to Identify Clients Who Need Tax Resolution

5 minutes

Recognizing clients with unresolved IRS tax debt who could benefit from professional resolution services.

1
Review tax returns for outstanding balances
When preparing or reviewing a client's tax return, check for any outstanding balances from the current or prior years. Look for amounts owed that total $7,000 or more across all tax periods. This is the minimum threshold for professional tax resolution to add meaningful value.
2
Ask about prior-year tax debt
Many clients have tax debt they have not mentioned. Ask directly: "Do you have any additional balances from prior years?" or "Have you received any IRS notices recently?" Clients often assume their tax preparer already knows about outstanding balances, or they feel embarrassed discussing them.
3
Look for red flags in the client's financial situation
Watch for indicators of unresolved tax issues: refunds being intercepted by the IRS, denial of Easy Advance loans, IRS notices or collection letters, wage garnishments, bank levies, or federal tax liens appearing on credit reports. Each of these signals active IRS collection on an outstanding balance.
4
Assess the client's ability and willingness to pay
Determine whether the client can pay the full balance within 4 months. If they cannot — or do not want to — they are a candidate for professional resolution. Clients who can pay in full within a short timeframe do not typically need resolution services.
5
Introduce the concept of tax resolution services
Keep the introduction simple and pressure-free: "We have partnered with one of the nation's largest tax resolution firms. They offer a free, no-obligation consultation to explain your options." Normalize the situation — millions of Americans owe back taxes. The initial consultation is confidential and commitment-free.

How to Submit a Referral

3 minutes

The process for submitting a client referral through the Community Tax partner portal.

1
Log in to the partner portal
Access the Community Tax partner portal using your partner credentials. If you do not have an account yet, apply at partners.communitytax.com — onboarding typically takes 1-2 business days.
2
Open the referral submission form
Click "Submit Referral" from the dashboard or navigation menu. The referral form takes approximately 2 minutes to complete.
3
Enter the client's basic information
Provide the client's full name, phone number, email address, and estimated total tax debt amount. Include any relevant notes about the client's situation — for example, whether they are facing active enforcement (garnishments, levies, liens) or have previously attempted self-resolution.
4
Submit and confirm
Review the information for accuracy and submit. You will receive a confirmation with a referral tracking number. Community Tax contacts the client within minutes during business hours.
5
Track the referral through the portal
Monitor your referral's status in the partner portal dashboard. You can see when the client has been contacted, when the Tax Investigation begins, and when the case moves to resolution. Revenue share is paid after the case is contracted and initial fees are collected.

How to Become a Partner

48 hours to full onboarding

The process for joining the Community Tax partner program.

1
Submit the partner application
Complete the online partner application at partners.communitytax.com. The application takes approximately 5 minutes and asks about your practice type, client volume, and how frequently you encounter clients with IRS tax debt. There is no fee to apply or join.
2
Schedule your introductory call
A partner manager will contact you within 1 business day to schedule a 15-minute introductory call. This call covers the program structure, helps you select the right partner tier (Direct, Enterprise, or Strategic), and sets revenue expectations based on your client base.
3
Review and sign the partner agreement
Review the partnership agreement, which covers revenue share rates, terms, payout schedules, and compliance responsibilities. All agreements are fully digital. Revenue share percentages range from 8% (Direct) to 18% (Strategic) depending on your tier.
4
Complete partner training
Complete a 30-minute training session covering how to identify clients with tax debt, how to make the referral conversation, and how to use the partner portal and AI tools (Revenue Lab, Script Builder, Ad Maker). Training can be completed on-demand.
5
Get portal access and submit your first referral
Receive login credentials for the partner portal, where you can submit referrals, track cases, view commissions, and access marketing materials. Submit your first referral through the portal — Community Tax contacts the client within minutes and the revenue share process begins.
