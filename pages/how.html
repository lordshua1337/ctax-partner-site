The Six Phases

Every partnership moves through a proven six-phase lifecycle.

1
Attract

Intake form, NDA, opportunity vetting and selection criteria review.

Attract
"Finally — a program that actually vets for fit."

You've seen partner programs that were really just sales pipelines dressed up as relationships. Our intake process filters for mutual fit first. If it's not right, we'll tell you — before anyone wastes time.

2
Engage

Program overview, agreements, team introductions, deal registration.

Engage
"No surprises after you've already committed."

Pricing, revenue share, what we need from your team — it all gets put on the table before you sign anything. Your board won't get blindsided. Neither will you.

3
Onboard

Training, SKO, sales alignment, competitive intel, co-selling setup.

Onboard
"Your team referring and converting — within weeks, not quarters."

Most partnerships take 6 months to generate their first dollar. Our SKO process is built around speed-to-revenue. You'll look like the person who brought in a program that actually worked.

4
Ramp

SPIFFs, contests, campaigns, webinars, events to accelerate referrals.

Ramp
"Other programs hand you a one-pager and call it enablement."

We run SPIFFs, contests, and live co-selling so your team actually moves. The difference between a referral program that sits on the shelf and one that drives revenue is execution support. We provide it.

5
Support

Bi-weekly check-ins, referral pipeline reviews, monthly reporting.

Support
"You don't have to become a tax expert. That's the point."

Bi-weekly check-ins and dedicated pipeline reviews mean we're carrying the operational weight. Your team stays focused on what they're already good at. We handle the complexity so you don't have to explain it.

6
Manage

QBRs, performance monitoring, forecasting, executive dashboards.

Manage
"If a prior referral program didn't work, it wasn't your clients."

It was a program with no accountability infrastructure. QBRs, real-time dashboards, forecasting — this is what it looks like when both sides are watching the numbers and answering for them.

Pre-Launch · 0–2 Months
Attract & Engage
  • Strategic fit assessment — market, capabilities, ROI
  • Agreements, pricing, and revenue share established
  • Branding and end-to-end sales process defined
  • Sales team alignment and product training complete
Launch · 2–4 Months
Enable & Ramp
  • Official Sales Kickoff (SKO) event
  • SPIFFs and contests to drive early referrals
  • Marketing campaigns and webinar series
  • Co-selling engagement with partner sales reps
Post-Launch · 5–18 Months
Support & Manage
  • Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)
  • Revenue monitoring and executive dashboards
  • Conflict management and continuous enablement
  • Assessment for program tier progression
Partner Selection Criteria

How we evaluate
partnership fit.

By selecting partners who deliver high value, pose minimal risk, are committed to collaboration, and can drive effective referral growth, CTAX optimizes for sustained success.

The Four Dimensions of Partner Fit

How Community Tax assesses each potential partnership before moving forward.

    Move the sliders to reflect where your organization is today — not where you want to be. The chart updates as you go, and we'll share what we think the fit looks like at the end. No right answers, just honest ones.

    Shared Opportunity 3

    A meaningful portion of your clients carry unresolved tax debt, and you have some structure for introducing third-party services — though it's not yet a formal process.

    Operational Alignment 3

    Your tech infrastructure is reasonably current, there are no major competitive conflicts, and your compliance posture is generally solid — though not fully documented at a partner level.

    Mutual Investment 3

    You're open to co-promoting the program and can assign someone to manage the relationship, but internal bandwidth is limited and you'd need a light-touch integration.

    Execution Capability 3

    Your team understands the space and has a working referral or cross-sell motion — you've done something like this before, even if not at full scale.
