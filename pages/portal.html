Quick Reference
How is my commission calculated?
Your commission is a percentage of the client's total tax debt, based on your tier. Silver tier earns 8%, Gold earns 10%, and Platinum earns 12-15%. Commission is paid when the case reaches "Resolved" status.
What happens after I submit a referral?
Our team contacts the client within 24 hours, pulls IRS transcripts, and begins investigation. You can track every step in the Referrals section. Average case resolution is 30-90 days depending on complexity.
How do I handle client objections?
The most common objection is cost. Remind clients that penalties and interest grow daily, and professional resolution often saves more than it costs. Our team handles all IRS communication — the client doesn't have to face the IRS alone.
What's the typical IRS resolution timeline?
Installment agreements: 30-60 days. Offers in Compromise: 6-12 months. Currently Not Collectible: 30-45 days. Penalty abatement: 30-60 days. Complex cases with multiple tax years may take longer.
Can I refer business clients (not just individuals)?
Yes. We handle both individual and business tax debt, including payroll tax issues, trust fund recovery penalties, and unfiled business returns. Business cases often have higher debt amounts, which means higher commissions for you.

How to Spot Tax Debt Clients Hiding in Your Practice

Most tax debt cases don't announce themselves. Clients are embarrassed, scared, or simply avoiding the problem. Here are the signals to watch for and the moments to pay attention.

Red Flag: Balance Due on Return
When you prepare a return and there's a balance due over $5,000, that's your opening. The client is already anxious. Say: "I want to make sure you have a plan for this balance. If it's something that's hard to pay right now, I work with a tax resolution team that can negotiate with the IRS on your behalf."
Red Flag: Missing Tax Years
A client who hasn't filed for 1+ years almost certainly has a compounding problem. When they come back, don't just file the returns -- ask: "Since we're catching up on these, would you like me to connect you with someone who can handle any balances or penalties with the IRS? They deal with this every day."
Red Flag: Client Mentions IRS Letters
If a client casually mentions "I got a letter from the IRS" or "they say I owe money," don't let it pass. This is your highest-conversion moment. Respond: "Do you have that letter? Let me look at it. Depending on what it says, I have a partner team that specializes in resolving these -- often for much less than the full amount."
Red Flag: Self-Employed with No Estimated Payments
Self-employed clients who don't make quarterly estimated payments accumulate debt fast. After calculating their liability, mention: "You've got about $X in estimated tax that's built up. Before it gets worse with penalties, I'd like to introduce you to a team that negotiates with the IRS. They can often set up a payment plan that actually works."
Red Flag: Divorce or Business Closure
Major life events create tax problems. Divorce splits often leave one spouse with the tax debt. Business closures leave payroll tax liabilities. Ask: "During transitions like this, tax issues often get lost in the shuffle. Want me to have my tax resolution partner do a quick check to make sure nothing is pending with the IRS?"
Red Flag: Client Asks About Payment Plans
When clients ask "Can I just pay the IRS monthly?" they're signaling they can't pay in full. This is a direct referral opportunity: "You can, but the IRS's default terms aren't always the best. I work with a resolution team that negotiates better terms -- sometimes they can reduce the total amount owed too. Want me to set up a free evaluation?"
Pro Tip: Add a "Tax Debt Check" item to your client meeting checklist. Partners who ask at every appointment generate 3-5x more referrals than those who only ask when they spot obvious problems.
Word-for-Word Scripts for Every Situation

The hardest part is knowing what to say. These scripts are tested across hundreds of partner practices. Adapt the language to your style, but keep the structure -- it works.

Easiest
The Routine Check-In
Use at: every appointment, end of meeting
"Before we wrap up -- are you fully current with the IRS? Any notices, old balances, or unfiled years I should know about? I ask everyone because I have a resource that can help, and most people don't realize how common this is."
Why it works: Normalizes the question ("I ask everyone"), removes shame, and positions you as helpful rather than nosy. Takes 15 seconds.
During Tax Prep
The Return Review Opener
Use at: when preparing returns with balance due
"So your return shows a balance due of $X. I know that can feel stressful. I want you to know that I work with a tax resolution team that handles exactly this -- they negotiate directly with the IRS and often get penalties reduced or set up manageable payment plans. Would you like me to connect you? There's no cost for the initial evaluation."
Why it works: Acknowledges the emotion, offers a solution immediately, and removes the cost objection upfront with "no cost for evaluation."
Casual / Social
The Networking Mention
Use at: networking events, dinners, casual conversations
"You know what's been surprising in my practice lately? The number of people who have old tax debt just sitting there, getting worse with penalties. I partnered with a resolution firm that handles it all -- IRS negotiation, penalty abatement, offers in compromise. If you ever run into someone in that situation, send them my way. I can get them a free evaluation."
Why it works: Non-salesy, positions you as knowledgeable, and plants the seed for future referrals without asking for anything directly.
Proactive Outreach
The Phone/Email Re-Engagement
Use with: clients who ghosted, old clients, past-due accounts
"Hi [Name], it's [You] from [Firm]. I was reviewing my client files and your name came up. I wanted to check in -- I know we haven't connected in a while, and I want to make sure there aren't any open tax issues that are building up. The IRS has been ramping up enforcement, and I've helped several clients recently get their situations resolved through a partner program. Is that something you'd want to explore?"
Why it works: Shows you care, creates urgency with "ramping up enforcement," and offers a warm path back without judgment about why they disappeared.
Third-Party
Referring on Behalf of Someone Else
Use when: a friend/family/colleague mentions someone with tax trouble
"I heard [Name] might be dealing with some IRS issues. I actually work with a tax resolution team -- they've settled cases for 20-40 cents on the dollar in some cases. It's completely free for them to get an evaluation. Would you mind passing along my number? Or I can reach out to them directly if you give me the OK."
Why it works: Third-party referrals feel more natural. The specific "20-40 cents on the dollar" creates immediate interest and gives the referrer a compelling reason to pass your info along.
Overcoming the 7 Most Common Objections

When a client pushes back, it usually means they're interested but scared. These responses are designed to acknowledge their concern and gently redirect to the solution.

"I can't afford to pay someone to help."
"I totally understand. Here's the thing -- the IRS is charging you penalties and interest every single month you wait. That debt is growing while we're talking. The initial evaluation is free, and the resolution fee often pays for itself by reducing what you owe. Would it hurt to at least get the free evaluation and see where you stand?"
Insight: This is the #1 objection. The key is reframing cost as an investment. The "growing while we're talking" line creates urgency without being pushy.
"I'll just handle it myself."
"You absolutely can -- and some people do. But the IRS has professional negotiators on their side, and they do this thousands of times a day. Having someone who knows the system on your side often gets a better outcome. Think of it like representing yourself in court vs. hiring an attorney. Let me at least get you the free evaluation so you can compare options."
Insight: Don't argue. Validate their capability, then use the courtroom analogy -- it clicks for most people. The "compare options" framing makes it low-risk.
"I don't trust those tax resolution companies."
"I hear you, and you're smart to be cautious. That's actually why I'm recommending this specific team -- I've vetted them personally. They're my partner, and I see the results for my clients. I wouldn't put my reputation on the line if they weren't legitimate. Want me to share some real case results?"
Insight: Your personal endorsement is the differentiator. You're not a TV ad -- you're their trusted advisor vouching for a specific team. Lean into that trust.
"I'll deal with it later / It's not that urgent."
"I used to think that too for clients, but here's what I've learned: every month you wait, the IRS adds penalties and interest -- usually 0.5-1% per month. On a $25,000 debt, that's $125-250 per month just in fees. Plus, the IRS can levy your bank account or garnish wages without warning once they've sent the required notices. The best time to resolve this is before they escalate."
Insight: Specifics beat vague urgency. The dollar-per-month calculation makes procrastination tangible. The levy/garnish mention adds real consequence.
"My debt isn't that bad / It's only $X."
"Even smaller balances can qualify for penalty abatement, which could save you 25-50% of what you owe. And stopping the penalty clock now prevents it from growing. The evaluation is free regardless of the amount -- there's no downside to checking."
Insight: Small debts still qualify for resolution. Penalty abatement alone can save thousands even on $10-15K balances. The "no downside" close works well here.
"I already have a payment plan with the IRS."
"That's good -- you've already taken a step. But did you know that many IRS payment plans aren't optimized? You might qualify for a lower monthly payment, or even a reduced total balance through an Offer in Compromise. It's worth having an expert review your plan to make sure you're not paying more than you need to."
Insight: Existing payment plans are often renegotiable. Clients don't know this. Position the resolution team as an optimizer, not a replacement for what they've already done.
"I need to think about it / talk to my spouse."
"Of course -- this is an important decision. Let me give you some information to share with [spouse]. And I'll follow up in a few days to see if you have any questions. In the meantime, the free evaluation offer stands whenever you're ready. No pressure at all."
Insight: Never push past this objection. Schedule a follow-up instead. Give them the Marketing Kit one-pager to take home. 40% of "I need to think about it" converts within 7 days with a follow-up.
Follow-Up Timing That Converts

The fortune is in the follow-up. Most referrals don't happen on the first conversation. Here's the exact sequence top-performing partners use.

1
Same Day
Send a Follow-Up Text or Email
Right after the conversation: "Hey [Name], great seeing you today. Here's the info on that tax resolution resource I mentioned -- [link to landing page]. They'll do a free evaluation, no strings attached. Let me know if you want me to make the intro." Short, warm, includes the link.
2
Day 3-4
Gentle Check-In
"Hi [Name], just checking in -- did you get a chance to look at that tax resolution info? Happy to answer any questions. No rush, just want to make sure you have everything you need." This is where 30% of conversions happen.
3
Day 7-10
Value-Add Touch
Don't ask again. Instead, share something useful: "Saw this article about IRS enforcement increasing this year -- thought of you. The resolution team I mentioned is seeing a lot of clients get ahead of this. Offer's still open whenever you're ready." Provides value, keeps the door open.
4
Day 21-30
Final Check-In (This Cycle)
"Hi [Name], just a final check-in on the tax situation we discussed. I'm here whenever you're ready. In the meantime, if you know anyone else dealing with IRS issues, I'd love to help them too." Pivots from the individual to a broader ask -- often triggers a referral even if they don't convert themselves.
5
Quarterly
Seasonal Re-Engagement
Add them to your quarterly outreach list. Touch points: tax season (Jan-Apr), extension deadline (Oct), year-end planning (Nov-Dec). Each seasonal email can reference current events: "IRS just announced new penalty rates for 2026..."
Key Metric: Top partners average 4.2 touches before a referral converts. Don't give up after one "no" -- persistence (without pressure) is what separates the top 10% of earners.

Your existing clients are your warmest leads, but a referral network multiplies your reach. These professionals encounter tax debt clients regularly and can become consistent referral sources.

Bankruptcy Attorneys
Every bankruptcy case involves a tax transcript review. Tax debt is often the trigger for filing. Approach: "When you see clients with IRS debt that can't be discharged in bankruptcy, I can connect them with a resolution team."
Conversion rate: Very High
Real Estate Agents
Tax liens prevent home sales and purchases. Agents encounter this regularly and need a fast resolution path. Approach: "When a client's deal is held up by a tax lien, I can get a resolution team involved to release it."
Conversion rate: High
Financial Advisors
Advisors see tax issues during financial planning reviews, especially with retirement distributions and investment gains. Approach: "When clients have tax liabilities affecting their financial plan, I have a resolution team that can help clean it up."
Conversion rate: Medium-High
Divorce Attorneys
Divorce splits assets and tax liabilities. Innocent spouse relief and joint liability are common. Approach: "Tax debt division is complicated in divorce. I can bring in a team to help your clients get proper resolution and potentially qualify for innocent spouse relief."
Conversion rate: High
Payroll Companies
Small businesses that fall behind on payroll taxes face Trust Fund Recovery Penalties. Payroll providers see this first. Approach: "When a client falls behind on payroll deposits, connect them with me before the IRS starts the TFRP assessment process."
Conversion rate: Medium
Mortgage Brokers
Tax liens and unfiled returns block mortgage approvals. Brokers need fast resolution to save their deals. Approach: "If a borrower is declined due to tax issues, I can get resolution started so you don't lose the deal. Most issues resolve in 30-60 days."
Conversion rate: High
Getting Started: Pick 2 professionals from the list above. Send each a brief email introducing yourself and the partnership. Offer to buy them coffee. One strong referral partner can generate 2-5 referrals per month consistently.

Knowledge Base
How do I update my payment method?
Navigate to the Payouts section in your portal sidebar. Click "Change Payment Method" under your current bank account. You'll need your routing number and account number. Changes take 1-2 business days to verify.
Why is my referral still showing "Investigation"?
Investigation typically takes 3-7 business days. During this time, our team is pulling IRS transcripts, reviewing the client's tax history, and determining the best resolution path. You'll receive an email when the case moves to Resolution status.
When will I receive my commission payout?
Commissions are processed on the 1st and 15th of each month. Your commission is released when the referred case reaches "Resolved" status. ACH transfers typically arrive within 2-3 business days after processing.
Can I refer a client who already has a tax attorney?
Yes, clients can switch representation at any time. We'll need a new Power of Attorney (Form 2848) signed by the client. Our team handles the transition professionally, and you still earn your full commission on the referral.
How do I access my 1099-NEC tax form?
Go to the Payouts section and scroll down to "Tax Documents." Your 1099-NEC for each year is available for download in PDF format. Forms for the current year are generated and made available by January 31st of the following year.
What if my client doesn't respond to Community Tax?
Our team makes multiple contact attempts over 7-10 days via phone, email, and text. If the client remains unresponsive, the referral moves to "Inactive" status. You can re-engage the client and resubmit at any time — the referral credit stays with you.
