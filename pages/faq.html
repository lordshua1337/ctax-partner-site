The Basics

What is the minimum tax balance you work with?

+

The general rule is that the most value is delivered with clients who owe at least $7,000 or more in total tax debt. This can be a combination of multiple tax years or a single year — the threshold applies to the total outstanding balance.

Does everyone qualify for a balance reduction (Offer in Compromise)?

+

No. Part of our two-phase approach is providing a transparent, expectation-setting experience. While we always look for debt reductions, not all clients will qualify. We explain this clearly on our initial call — we never make promises we can't keep.

What does this cost the client?

+

Each client is contracted for a Tax Investigation fee of $295 (businesses add $500). After the investigation, we present a flat cost to resolve their liability. Approximately 80% of investigated clients elect to proceed. We offer discounts and payment plans so we don't turn away clients due to inability to pay.

Do you offer any guarantee?

+

Yes — a money-back Service Guarantee. If the Investigation is complete and we cannot offer any qualifying services, we refund the Investigation fee in full. Note: this is a service guarantee, not a satisfaction guarantee.

Will my clients keep their tax preparation with me?

+

Yes — always. We provide resolution services only. Partners who don't refer often lose that prep work anyway when clients find resolution help elsewhere — and those firms then solicit the future preparation. Referring us protects your prep revenue.

What states does the program cover?

+

We operate in 48 states — nationwide with the exception of Minnesota and North Dakota. For state tax balances, we currently work with California and New York exclusively.

Revenue & Compensation

How does partner revenue share work?

+

Partners earn a percentage of the fees Community Tax collects on referred cases. The exact revenue share percentage is established in your partner agreement and scales with your tier — Direct, Enterprise, or Strategic. All terms are disclosed before you sign anything.

What does the average case pay a partner?

+

Resolution cases vary significantly in complexity and total fees — simpler installment agreement cases tend to be on the lower end, while Offer in Compromise cases can command substantially higher fees. Revenue share on a single closed case can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars. Your partner manager will walk through realistic expectations based on your client profile.

When do I get paid?

+

Revenue share is paid after the referred client's case is contracted and initial fees are collected — not after full resolution. You do not have to wait 12–18 months for an OIC to close before you receive compensation. Payout schedules are confirmed during onboarding.

Is there a minimum referral volume to stay active as a partner?

+

Direct partners have no minimum volume requirements. Enterprise and Strategic partners agree to volume commitments as part of their tier structure — these are established collaboratively based on your client base and are designed to be achievable, not punitive.

Are there any fees to join the partner program?

+

No. There is no cost to become a Community Tax partner at any tier. We earn when you earn — our incentive is to make your referral program as productive as possible.

How do I track what I've earned?

+

A partner portal with real-time referral tracking and revenue reporting is in active development. Until launch, your dedicated partner manager provides regular pipeline updates and revenue statements. Enterprise and Strategic partners receive monthly reporting as part of their tier.

Identifying Clients

Who is a target client?

+

Start with tax returns you've prepared that have outstanding balances — and ask: "Do you have any additional balances from prior years?" The ideal client owes $7,000+ and cannot or does not want to pay it back in full or within 4 months.

What about clients who've tried to fix this on their own?

+

This is an ideal referral. Resolving IRS problems independently is tedious and complex. Clients who have tried and failed are often the most motivated to accept professional help.

What about clients facing active enforcement — notices, garnishments, levies?

+

Excellent referrals. We can explain threatening notices, work with local IRS agents, and obtain garnishment and wage levy releases. Active enforcement creates urgency — these clients are highly motivated to engage immediately.

Can we help undocumented clients with tax problems?

+

Yes. The IRS and ICE are completely separate agencies. Fixing tax problems is essential on the path to documentation and citizenship — and our bilingual team serves Spanish-speaking clients with the same expertise.

What if the IRS keeps my client's refund every year?

+

If the IRS is intercepting annual refunds or a client has been denied an Easy Advance, there is almost certainly a back tax liability. These are strong indicators to initiate a referral. Note: Community Tax does not handle stimulus payments or refund status inquiries.

The Process

When does Community Tax reach out after a referral?

+

As soon as you submit the client's information through the referral portal. During business hours, we aim to make contact within minutes. Speed drives conversion, and we move fast on every referral.

How long does the tax resolution process take?

+

Phase 1 (Tax Investigation) averages 1–7 business days. Phase 2 (Resolution) varies — some agreements can be in place in 7–10 days; an Offer in Compromise can take up to 18 months. We set proper expectations after the Investigation is complete.

My clients trust me — how will I stay involved?

+

We prefer to work directly with clients to take the load off you, but you own the relationship. As the referring partner, you have dedicated support channels at your discretion. A client portal is in development for real-time referral tracking.

Why shouldn't I just handle this myself?

+

Non-credentialed tax professionals have limited representation rights — they cannot handle appeals or collection matters. EAs, Tax Attorneys, and CPAs have unlimited representation rights. This is what we do for thousands of clients every month. Your time is more valuable spent on returns and growing your client base.

IRS Programs

What IRS programs do clients qualify for?

+

Programs are determined by the client's income, assets, and expenses. Options include Installment Agreements, Currently Not Collectible status, Offer in Compromise (settlement), Penalty Abatement, and more. The Tax Investigation phase pre-qualifies the client for the best possible outcome.

What is the "Fresh Start Program" and "People's First Initiative"?

+

These were IRS internal guideline adjustments designed to add flexibility to program qualification criteria, speed up approval times, and encourage taxpayers to resolve their tax matters.

Does Community Tax handle back state taxes?

+

We currently work with state balances from California and New York exclusively. State authorities are often harder to reach and less cooperative than the IRS — we only offer services we can reliably deliver.
